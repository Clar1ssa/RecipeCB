History of a Cheeseburger: The Variation of the original hamburgers history is quite interesting! Going back as far as the 1920's a young chef named Lionel Sternberger is said to have simply added a slice of cheese to a hamburger at his dads restaurant in Pasadena, California. Although there are many who claim to also have been the first to add cheese to the hamburger. Eventually in the 1930s and 40's as fast food began to evolve, cheeseburgers were a go to on their menus. There are many variations of the cheeseburger and the concept of adding cheese as an enhancement was a hit ! Today cheeseburgers are enjoyed globally and the claim to there origin is is still questioned to this day.

Cook Time: 25 minutes

Serves: 4

Recipe:

  1. Prep Ingredients:
  2. Clean any vegetables intended to be used : Onion,Lettuce and Tomato and slice them to thickness desired.put to the side.
  3. Next place ground beef in bowl or work space desired and season with desired seasonings and mix.
  4. Divide ground beef into 4 equal parts since we are making 4 burgers; making them into round balls then smashing them flat with the palm of your hand is a method used to get that "patty-like" shape.
  5. Heat cooking oil (just enough to coat the pan) in medium to large skillet
  6. Add your 2-4 patties flat into the heated pan with the oil
  7. Cook time will be based on the thickness of the meat, but once easily able to be lifted from pan with spatula - go ahead and flip them.
  8. Cook them on both sides to desired "wellness"
  9. Add Patty to Bun
  10. Add Cheese to Patty
  11. Add Lettuce Tomato and Onions (or desired toppings) like the pickles or even enjoy thm on the side
  12. Close the bun
  13. Enjoy!
To spice things up, additional toppings like peppers, bacon,fried eggs, onion rings or pineapple can also spice up a cheeseburger; as well as doubling up the patties, using a "lettuce " bun or even adding french fries to the burger- whatever you'd like !
